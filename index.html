Telescope
The **Kepler Space Telescope**
was a groundbreaking space observatory launched by NASA in 2009,
dedicated to discovering Earth-like planets orbiting other
stars in our galaxy.
Named after the renowned astronomer Johannes Kepler,
this telescope revolutionized our
understanding of the universe
by focusing on a small patch of the Milky Way
to detect planets through the transit method—observing tiny dips in a star's brightness
as a planet passes in front of it.

**Key Achievements:**
- **Exoplanet Discoveries**: Kepler identified over 2,600 confirmed exoplanets,
many of which are potentially habitable and similar in size to Earth.
- **Expanding the Hunt**: It revealed that planets are common in the galaxy,
with billions potentially orbiting in the habitable zones of their stars.
- **Legacy**: The mission concluded in 2018, but Kepler’s data
continues to provide insights, shaping our search for life beyond Earth
and guiding future missions.

Kepler’s discoveries have fundamentally changed our view of our place in the universe,
showing that Earth-like planets may be more common than once thought,
and paving the way for future explorations of distant worlds.
